  • Alexia Perineau

    Cultivating Authenticity and Connection in Startups

  • Alexia gives team building workshops to deepen connection between people.

  • Why

    Startups are changing the way people work, towards more equality and well being, through transforming the system. I believe inner changes within people are essential to accompany this amazing shift.

     

    It is only through freedom to be, amongst others, that both creativity and happiness will emerge.

     

    According to Google’s Aristotle study, the 2 most determinant factors for a successful team are that all members have equal contribution to conversations, and that they are mindful of one another’s emotions. The workshops are made to work on these two aspects, bringing people to feel confortable with one another, and caring for each other.

    How

    During workshops, people relate through other ways than conventional conversation: they share silence, laughter, physical connection, authentic exchange of words. An atmosphere of non judgement is key.

    What

    The first step is connection to self through movement and meditation. Self knowledge is a useful practice for deeper motivation, finding one’s real talents and aspirations. It also constitutes an essential base for connecting to others.

     

    Then comes connection to others through connected meditations and physical exercices to work on trust and deep listening.

     

    The third stage is to come back to words to explore new understandings of self and others.

     

    I also accompany the group into implementing new practices for their company, to keep this work of connection going.

     

  • About

    Passionate about startups, their open mind, creative energy, and how they are shaping the world of tomorrow, as she discovered different practices and ways to connect through emerging yoga and dance communities, she embodies a link between those two worlds.

     

    She is inspired by Yoga, Partner yoga, Contact Improvisation, Nonviolent Communication and Unschooling principles, and her multicultural background, having spent time in San Francisco, London, Paris, South India including Auroville, Buenos Aires, Tehran and Berlin.

     

    Her formal education includes a Master in Bioengineering from Imperial College and a Yoga Teacher Certificate from Indea Yoga School.
